# How to Rank in AI Search (New Strategy & Framework)

## Executive Summary

- AI search is reshaping digital visibility: brands must win both mentions (seen) and citations (trusted) to maximize conversions.
- The Seen & Trusted Framework coordinates cross-team efforts for AI visibility.
- Playbook 1: Get Seen—win favorable mentions in AI answers by optimizing reviews, community engagement, UGC, and list inclusions.
- Playbook 2: Be Trusted—earn citations as a reliable source through technical SEO, transparent data, documentation, and original research.
- Run both playbooks in parallel for compounding results.

---

## Why AI Search Strategy Isn’t Just SEO’s Job

AI search engines like ChatGPT, Google AI Mode, and Perplexity pull signals from everywhere—not just your website. Winning in AI search requires cross-departmental coordination: SEO, PR, product, support, and community teams all play a role. Without synchronization, strong performance in one area can be undermined by weakness in another.

### Real-World Example: The Silo Trap

Consider a SaaS company with a world-class SEO team but a disengaged support team. Their site ranks well, but their documentation is outdated and their product is rarely discussed on Reddit or G2. When ChatGPT is asked about the best tools in their category, the brand is either omitted or described with outdated, negative sentiment. Meanwhile, a competitor with average SEO but strong community engagement and up-to-date docs is both mentioned and cited. The lesson: AI search rewards brands that coordinate across all digital touchpoints.

### What AI Actually Cites
- Review platforms (G2, Capterra, Yelp, Google Reviews)
- Community discussions (Reddit, Stack Overflow, Quora)
- News coverage and analyst reports
- Support docs and technical documentation
- Social proof and user-generated content

---

## The Seen & Trusted (S&T) Framework

A systematic approach to maximize both brand mentions and citations in AI answers.

### Framework Overview

The S&T Framework is built on two pillars:

1. **Get Seen:** Win favorable mentions in AI answers by building a strong presence across review sites, communities, and social platforms.
2. **Be Trusted:** Earn citations as a reliable, authoritative source by optimizing your site’s technical foundation, publishing transparent data, and creating original research.

Each pillar contains actionable steps, technical tactics, and measurement strategies. Let’s break them down.

### Get Seen (Sentiment Battle)
- Win favorable mentions in AI-generated answers—even without a citation link.
- Focus on review quality, community engagement, UGC, and list inclusions.

### Be Trusted (Authority Game)
- Earn citations as a reliable, authoritative source.
- Optimize technical SEO, transparency, documentation, and original research.

## Playbook 1: How to Get Seen (The Sentiment Battle)

### 1. Build Presence on the Right Review Sites
- Target platforms relevant to your industry (G2, Capterra, Amazon, Yelp, Google Reviews).
- Prioritize detailed, substantive reviews over quantity.
- Incentivize customers for in-depth feedback; respond to every review.

#### Deep Dive: Engineering Review Quality

AI models weigh the *substance* of reviews, not just the star rating. A review that details onboarding experience, feature usage, and outcomes is far more valuable than dozens of “Great product!” comments. For example, Slack’s G2 reviews often mention specific integrations and workflow automations, making them a go-to source for AI answers.

**Action Steps:**
- Send review requests after key milestones (e.g., onboarding, support resolution).
- Use prompts: “How did our API save you time?”
- Offer incentives for detailed feedback (e.g., credits for reviews over 200 words).
- Respond to every review—AI recognizes vendor engagement as a trust signal.

#### Case Study: B2B SaaS Review Strategy

A mid-market SaaS brand increased its AI mentions by 40% in six months by launching a “review sprint” campaign. They targeted power users, provided review templates, and publicly responded to every review. The result: more detailed, positive reviews and a measurable uptick in AI-generated brand mentions.

### 2. Participate in Community Discussions
- Engage authentically on Reddit, Quora, Stack Overflow, and LinkedIn.
- Answer questions, address misconceptions, and share product roadmaps.
- Encourage technical and support teams to participate directly.

#### Advanced Tactics: Community Engagement

AI search engines heavily weight authentic, helpful participation in forums. Brands like Tally and Zoho have grown their share of voice by:
- Having founders and engineers answer technical questions on Reddit.
- Addressing negative threads transparently.
- Sharing product roadmaps and feature updates in public forums.

**Pro Tip:** Don’t just answer questions about your own product. Engage in broader industry discussions to build topical authority.

#### Example: Turning a Negative Thread Positive

When a competitor’s product was criticized on Reddit, their support lead joined the thread, acknowledged the issue, and shared a roadmap for improvement. This transparency shifted sentiment and led to positive AI mentions in subsequent queries.

### 3. Engineer UGC and Social Proof
- Encourage customers to share wins, case studies, and creative use cases on social platforms.
- Partner with micro-influencers for authentic product stories.
- Reward public feedback and reply to every mention.

#### Building a Social Proof Flywheel

User-generated content (UGC) is a feedback loop that AI amplifies. Patagonia, for example, dominates “sustainable outdoor brands” queries because customers regularly share stories about their exchange policy and product quality on Reddit and Instagram.

**Action Steps:**
- Launch a “Share Your Story” campaign with rewards for creative use cases.
- Feature customer stories in your newsletter and on your site.
- Partner with micro-influencers for authentic, niche content.
- Reply to every public mention—AI recognizes visible engagement.

#### Example: UGC in Action

An e-commerce brand saw a 30% increase in AI mentions after running a “before and after” contest, encouraging customers to post results on LinkedIn and Instagram. The resulting UGC was cited in multiple AI-generated product recommendations.

### 4. Secure “Best of” List Inclusions

Third-party listicles are a primary source for AI citations. Brands like Garmin and TechRadar consistently appear in “best GPS watch” and “top project management tools” articles, which are then cited by AI engines.

**Action Steps:**
- Build a press kit page with specs, pricing, and high-res images.
- Reach out to journalists before annual list updates.
- Pitch for both core and adjacent categories (e.g., “best for remote teams”).
- Track listicle inclusions and update assets regularly.

#### Example: Expanding Category Reach

Garmin doesn’t just appear in “best GPS watch” lists—they’re also featured in broader outdoor and fitness roundups. This multiplies their citation opportunities across AI platforms.

## Playbook 2: How to Be Trusted (The Authority Game)

### 1. Optimize Your Official Site for AI
- Use semantic HTML and structured data (JSON-LD, schema.org).
- Keep critical information visible and accessible (no JS-only content).
- Pass Core Web Vitals and ensure fast, stable, crawlable pages.

#### Technical Deep Dive: Semantic Infrastructure

AI engines extract facts from structured data. Use JSON-LD for organization, product, FAQ, and HowTo schema. Mark up pricing tables, feature lists, and reviews with native HTML elements. Avoid hiding key data behind tabs, accordions, or JavaScript.

**Checklist:**
- Use <table>, <ul>, <h2> for key data.
- Place critical info (meta tags, canonical, schema) at the top of your HTML.
- Test with Google’s Rich Results and Mobile-Friendly tools.

#### Example: Schema-Driven Citations

Bankrate and InStyle are frequently cited in AI answers because their pages are fast, well-structured, and rich in schema markup. Their pricing and product data is easily extractable by LLMs.

### 2. Maintain Wikipedia & Knowledge Graph Accuracy
- Audit and update your Wikipedia page quarterly.
- Claim and optimize your Google Knowledge Panel.
- Add or improve Organization schema and Wikidata entries.

#### How to Build and Maintain Authority

Wikipedia and Google’s Knowledge Graph are foundational for AI trust. Ensure your Wikipedia page is up-to-date, neutral, and well-cited. Use the “Talk” page to propose factual corrections. For the Knowledge Graph, claim your panel and update your Organization schema and Wikidata entry.

**Action Steps:**
- Audit Wikipedia quarterly for outdated info.
- Add third-party citations for all claims.
- Claim your Knowledge Panel and verify details.
- Update schema and Wikidata with new products or leadership changes.

#### Example: Fixing a Knowledge Graph Error

A fintech company discovered its Knowledge Panel listed an outdated CEO. By updating their Organization schema and Wikidata, and proposing a Wikipedia edit, they corrected the error—improving AI trust and citation rates.

### 3. Publish Transparent Pricing
- Make pricing, tiers, and feature comparisons public and machine-readable.
- Update pricing on review platforms and your own site.

#### Why Transparency Wins

AI engines penalize brands with hidden or ambiguous pricing. When users can’t find pricing, AI pulls speculative data from Reddit or LinkedIn, often resulting in negative sentiment. Transparent, well-structured pricing pages are cited as authoritative sources.

**Checklist:**
- Publish tier breakdowns and feature comparisons.
- List annual vs. monthly options.
- Update pricing on G2, Capterra, and your site.

#### Example: Turning Pricing Transparency into a Competitive Edge

A SaaS brand moved from “Contact Sales” to public pricing and saw a 25% increase in positive AI citations. Their pricing page became a go-to source for value-comparison queries.

### 4. Expand Documentation & FAQs
- Create dedicated, crawlable pages for integrations, troubleshooting, and FAQs.
- Use static HTML, XML sitemaps, and schema markup for docs.

#### Documentation as a Citation Magnet

Support docs and FAQs are among the most-cited sources in technical AI answers. Apple’s support pages, for example, are frequently referenced for troubleshooting queries.

**Action Steps:**
- Build dedicated pages for each integration and common issue.
- Use HowTo and FAQ schema.
- Ensure docs are crawlable and included in XML sitemaps.

#### Example: Scaling Documentation for AI Visibility

Dialpad created individual integration pages for every major app, each with step-by-step guides and schema markup. This led to a surge in AI citations for integration-related queries.

### 5. Create Original Research
- Publish proprietary data, benchmark studies, and industry surveys.
- Lead with key findings, methodology, and downloadable datasets.
- Promote research through press releases and industry coverage.

#### The Power of Proprietary Data

Original research is the gold standard for AI citations. SentinelOne, for example, is both frequently mentioned and cited because it publishes unique threat reports and benchmark studies.

**Checklist:**
- Conduct annual surveys with 500+ respondents.
- Publish benchmark studies with downloadable data.
- Promote findings via press and industry outlets.

#### Example: Research-Driven Authority

After publishing a “State of the Industry” report, a cybersecurity firm was cited by Forbes, TechCrunch, and multiple AI engines. Their unique data became the default reference for key queries.

## Pulling It All Together: Running Both Playbooks

### 1. Audit Your Current AI Visibility
- Run test queries in ChatGPT, Google AI Mode, and Perplexity.
- Track mentions (in answers) and citations (in source lists).
- Use tools like Semrush Enterprise AIO for competitor benchmarking.

#### Step-by-Step Audit Process

1. List your top 10 brand, product, and category queries.
2. Run each in ChatGPT, Google AI Mode, and Perplexity.
3. Screenshot answers, noting mentions and citations.
4. Track competitors’ visibility for the same queries.
5. Identify gaps: Are you mentioned but not cited? Cited but not mentioned?

**Pro Tip:** Use a spreadsheet or dashboard to track monthly changes and spot trends.

### 2. Build Parallel Campaigns
- Coordinate review, community, PR, product, and SEO teams.
- Create a shared dashboard to track AI visibility progress.

#### Cross-Functional Playbook

Assign clear roles:
- Customer success: Drives review campaigns.
- Community: Engages in forums and social platforms.
- PR: Secures “best of” list inclusions and media coverage.
- Product: Publishes transparent pricing and documentation.
- SEO: Optimizes site structure and schema.

Hold monthly syncs to review progress and adjust tactics.

### 3. Monitor and Iterate
- Track mentions and citations monthly.
- Adjust focus based on strengths and weaknesses (sentiment vs. authority).
- Reverse-engineer competitor jumps in AI visibility.

#### Advanced Monitoring Tools

Use LLM tracking tools like Semrush Enterprise AIO, or build a manual prompt list to monitor your brand’s AI visibility. Watch for sudden changes in competitor visibility and investigate the cause—new PR, more reviews, or updated documentation.

**Pitfall to Avoid:** Don’t rely solely on rankings or traffic. Focus on GSOV and citation share for a true measure of AI search success.

## The Evolution of AI Search: From Blue Links to Generative Answers

The search landscape has undergone a seismic shift. Where once the goal was to rank #1 in a list of blue links, today’s AI-powered engines like ChatGPT, Google AI Mode, and Perplexity deliver synthesized, conversational answers. Instead of ten blue links, users get a single, authoritative response—often with cited sources and brand mentions woven directly into the answer.

### Why This Matters

This change means that traditional SEO tactics—keyword stuffing, backlink chasing, and even classic on-page optimization—are no longer enough. AI search engines pull from a much broader set of signals: reviews, forums, news, documentation, and even social sentiment. Brands must now optimize for both *visibility* (being mentioned) and *trust* (being cited as a source).

### The New Metrics: GSOV and Citation Share

Success in AI search is measured by Generative Share of Voice (GSOV)—how often your brand appears in AI-generated answers—and Citation Share, the frequency with which your site is referenced as a trusted source. These metrics require a holistic, cross-functional approach.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I check my brand’s AI visibility?**
A: Run queries in ChatGPT, Google AI Mode, and Perplexity for your brand, category, and product. Note where you’re mentioned and cited. Use tools like Semrush Enterprise AIO for deeper analysis.

**Q: What’s more important: mentions or citations?**
A: Both are critical. Mentions drive visibility; citations build trust. The most successful brands win on both fronts.

**Q: How often should I update my strategy?**
A: Monitor monthly. AI search evolves rapidly—adjust campaigns as new platforms and citation patterns emerge.

**Q: Can small brands compete in AI search?**
A: Yes. Authentic engagement, transparent data, and original research can help smaller brands win both mentions and citations—even against larger competitors.

**Q: What are the biggest pitfalls in AI search optimization?**
A: The most common mistakes are focusing only on classic SEO, neglecting review and community signals, hiding pricing, and failing to update documentation. AI search rewards brands that are transparent, engaged, and technically sound.

**Q: How do I get cited by AI if I’m a new brand?**
A: Start by building authority through third-party reviews, active community participation, and publishing unique research or data. Even small brands can become go-to sources if they provide value others don’t.

**Q: How do I measure GSOV and citation share?**
A: Track how often your brand is mentioned and cited in AI-generated answers for your target queries. Use tools or manual tracking to benchmark against competitors.

**Q: Should I use AI to generate my own content?**
A: Yes, but focus on quality and originality. Use AI to scale unique, data-driven content, not to churn out generic articles. Always add human insight and review.
